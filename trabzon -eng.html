  • Sumela Monastery

    The Sümela Monastery, built on a steep cliff in the foothills of Montenegro, dominating the Altındere valley, within the borders of Altındere Village of Maçka District of Trabzon, is known as the "Virgin Mary" among the people. The structure, which is about 300 meters above the valley, has continued the tradition of establishing monasteries outside the city, in forests, caves and waterfront. It is said that the monastery, founded in the name of the Virgin Mary, took its name "Sumela" from the word "molasses" meaning "black".

  • Hamsikoy

    Don't be fooled by the name Hamsiköy, which is located on the foothills of the Zigana Mountains at an altitude of 1300 meters, this place is famous for its rice pudding, not its anchovies. Its name comes from "hamse", which means 5 in Arabic. In other words, the original name of the village is Hamsköy or Hamseköy. It is a collective name given to five villages in this region. These five villages: Hamsiköy, Ciharlı, Çıralı, Dikkaya and Güzelyayla.
