Nature Sports in Mersin

    Skiing: There is a ski facility on the northern slopes of Bolkar Mountain between Niğde and Mersin. Mersin-Bolkar Mountains Bolkar Mountains, which form the wing of the Alp mountains in Turkey, start from Reşadiye in the southwest and extend parallel to the southern shores of Anatolia and connect to the Zağanos Mountains of Iran in the east. There are many peaks exceeding 3000 meters in Bolkar mountains. Medetsiz (3524 m.) Is the highest among them. Meydan is a very good camping spot for those who want to see the Bolkar mountains. Karagöl (2650 m.), Located in the southwest, about 45 minutes from the square, is a very beautiful camping spot and if you go here in June, it can be seen that the lake is surrounded by yellow flowers.   • Kanlidivane Antik Kenti

    • Kanytella, today known as Kanlıdivane, is located in the inner part of the mountain side 3 kilometers after Eliaussa Sebaste (Ayaş). The settlement, which covers the edge of a sinkhole (about 170 X 200 meters in size), which is 60 meters deep and descended by steps carved into the rocks, was connected to the Olba Dynasty in the 2nd century BC, as the inscriptions document, and even served as the port of Olba to the sea. Based on the geographic similarity with the Korykon Antron (Heaven-Hell), there is a Hellenistic sanctuary inside the pothole. The tower, built of polygonal blocks on the south edge of the pothole, was built by Priest Prince Tuekros, son of Tarkyaris at the beginning of the 2nd century BC and was dedicated to Zeus Olbios. There are reliefs of the Armaronxas family on the south wall inside the pothole. The relief, located in a 4x2 meter niche, has a somewhat rough workmanship. There is a five-line inscription on the right side of the relief and the names of the family are included here. The family consists of six people, the father and mother live next to their four children. One of them is boy and three of them are girls.

  • Adamkayalar

    • Adam Kayalar, estimated to have been built between the 3rd century BC and the 3rd century AD, is located in Şeytan Deresi Valley, which is about 5 kilometers from Erdemli's Kızkalesi Neighborhood. The archaeological ruin, known as Adam Kayalar and consisting of large reliefs, is one of the most important places in Mersin. The reliefs, estimated to have been processed in 250 years, await visitors on a valley slope that is difficult to access. There are figures of 4 women, 11 men, two children, a mountain goat and a Roman eagle in 9 niches.
